Violin


All it takes is a dainty instrument and a wispy bow to come in contact for some melodious hooks. Since its birth in the 16th century. The cry of a fiddle has managed to touch, and sympathise souls through the generations. From Nicolo Paganini and Pablo De Sarasate to Lindsey Stirling, David Garet and Bryson Andres. They evolved phrases of the violin to bind people with their incomprehensible emotions through music. Fingering, Posture formation and bowing forms are techniques to be followed to reach their kind of majesticity. This instrument is involved in genres like Western Classical, Jazz, Country and Bluegrass. With a strong base, commitment, determination and your enthusiasm, we will aid your experience of expressing yourself through the Vioin.


Areas of Focus


In these sessions, you will learn several techniques of violin playing, including learning and relaxing into the posture, playing an open string with a long bow, playing a rhythm, placing fingers on the strings, learning basic finger positioning on strings, playing simple melody, new and more complex finger positionings, Pizzicato, Bowing Techniques and Dynamics (staccato, legato, dynamics), slurs with scales, arpeggios and melodies, introducing 4th finger, introducing minor scales and arpeggios, and harmonic & melodic finger positioning.


Note


A decent bow is imperative - a good wooden bow handcarved from a block of pernambuco with a fully mounted frog and a bone tip. There are several makers we recommend including Horst John (brand, not maker), Arcos Brasil (also a brand), Richard Rigall (American maker).
Daily practice is important following the instructions from your teacher, practice a minimum of one hour a day. Scales and arpeggios are the most important, and an excellent way to warm up.
